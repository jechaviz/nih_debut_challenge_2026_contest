# Competitive Landscape

## Why RenalCue is not just another calculator

| Alternative | Strength | Gap | RenalCue wedge |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| eGFR calculators | Fast and familiar | Single value, no workflow action | Adds UACR, BP context, validation and route |
| KFRE web tools | Validated kidney-failure risk | Often separate from intake workflow | Creates a community-screening handoff receipt |
| EHR alerts | Integrated with patient record | Needs EHR access and can be ignored | Runs as standalone prototype for low-resource review |
| Paper referral forms | Simple and cheap | Manual, variable and hard to audit | Structured evidence receipt with deterministic logic |
| Generic triage apps | Broad symptom coverage | Weak kidney-specific prize fit | Built around NIDDK-relevant CKD markers |

## Defensible wedge

RenalCue wins if reviewers see it as a workflow safety system: transparent,
CKD-specific, low-cost, auditable and easy for undergraduate teams to validate.
It should not compete as a novel eGFR formula or a black-box AI model.

## IP posture

The immediate protectable assets are original code, workflow design,
documentation, test fixtures, receipt format and brand. Patentability should be
reviewed only after the eligible team and institution clarify ownership.
