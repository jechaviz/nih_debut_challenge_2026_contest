# Competition Strategy

Status: `v1.3_competitive_upgrade`

## Competitive reality

DEBUT is crowded. NIH reported that the 2025 challenge had 123 teams, 534
students and 67 universities. A generic software dashboard will not stand out.
RenalCue must look like a biomedical design project with a sharp unmet need,
working prototype, believable low-resource deployment and disciplined safety
boundary.

## Judge thesis

RenalCue should be judged as an engineering system for kidney-risk workflow
reliability, not as another health app. It turns a fragile community screening
handoff into an auditable receipt: input validation, eGFR, UACR category, risk
band, escalation route and clinician handoff packet.

## Winning angles

- NIDDK fit: kidney disease is the central clinical target, not a side example.
- Low-resource fit: runs as static web plus Vlang CLI; no cloud account or PHI is
  required for demonstration.
- Undergraduate fit: transparent rules are explainable by a student team and can
  be validated with faculty/clinician advisors.
- Prototype fit: reviewers can run it, inspect cases and verify receipts.
- Safety fit: it is decision support for clinician review, not autonomous
  diagnosis.
- VentureWell fit: low-cost adoption path for clinics, student outreach programs
  and safety-net screening workflows.

## What must be added by the team before final upload

- One clinician or nurse workflow interview.
- One faculty sponsor paragraph confirming student ownership.
- One figure showing before/after workflow time.
- One stakeholder letter from a clinic, outreach program or kidney advocate.
- One 3-minute video that shows the working demo and the generated evidence.
