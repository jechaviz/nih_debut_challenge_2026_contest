## Eligibility

- Student team must include 3-8 undergraduate students.
- Each student must be enrolled full-time in a U.S. undergraduate curriculum for
  at least one full semester or quarter in academic year 2025-2026.
- Team captain must be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident.
- If the institution has a biomedical engineering or bioengineering department,
  at least one student must major in biomedical engineering or bioengineering.
- Each entry must be conceived, designed and implemented by the student team.
- Each team member signs the NIBIB DEBUT Certification Form.

## Required submission artifacts

- One single PDF submission.
- Certification form with project title, team member information, citizenship or
  permanent-resident indication, dates and signatures.
- Project narrative, max 6 pages, Arial font size at least 11.
- Faculty sponsor letter on department letterhead.
- Parental consent form if required.
- Required 3-minute video link.
- Optional separate material: up to three stakeholder letters, up to three
  background articles/reports and up to three extra links.

## Required project narrative sections

- Abstract.
- Clinical need or problem, background and current methods.
- Project objective and innovative aspects.
- Design documentation with sufficient detail.
- Prototype documentation, evidence of function and evaluation results.
- 3-minute video link.

## Prize fit

- Primary: NIDDK Kidney Technology Development Prize.
- Secondary: Healthcare technologies for underserved populations or low-resource
  settings.
- Additional fit: community nursing workflows if a nurse-led intake partner is
  documented.

## Accessibility and safety

- Submission electronic content should meet Section 508 accessibility standards.
- Software is positioned as clinician-facing decision support. Intended use,
  limitations and FDA CDS risk should remain explicit in the narrative.
